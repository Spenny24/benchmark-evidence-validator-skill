# Excluded Benchmarks — Example

Synthetic example for illustration only, paired with `example-benchmark-register.csv`. These claims were reviewed during the same intake pass and did not clear the bar for `Internal challenge only`, so they do not appear in the register at all.

---

## Claim 1 — "AI writing tools cut content costs by 90%"

**Original claim:** "AI writing tools cut content costs by 90%." (submitted by the user as a pasted note, no further context given)

**Reason excluded:** No source of any kind, primary or secondary, could be identified for this figure. No organisation, report, or even a general originating claim pattern to point to — it reads as an assertion rather than a reported finding.

**Missing fields:** source_organisation, source_title, source_url, publication_date, industry_scope, functional_scope. Also, a 90% cost reduction is a large enough claim that it would need strong sourcing before it could be treated as even directionally useful internally.

**What would promote this:** A named study, vendor report, or case study stating this figure, with a way to check what "content costs" and "cut" actually mean in the original context (all-in production cost? Just drafting time? One case study or an aggregate?). Given the size of the claimed effect, this would likely need Primary verified evidence status to be usable even internally, not just Secondary/internal.

## Claim 2 — "Our platform customers see 10x ROI in the first quarter"

**Original claim:** "Our platform customers see 10x ROI in the first quarter." (found on a software vendor's product marketing page)

**Reason excluded:** This is vendor marketing copy, not a benchmark finding. No methodology, sample, timeframe definition, or named study backs the figure, and vendor-published ROI claims about their own product carry an inherent conflict of interest that a blank citation can't offset.

**Missing fields:** source_title (a marketing page is not a study), publication_date, metric definition (ROI calculated how — cost savings? revenue? against what baseline?), functional_scope.

**What would promote this:** An independent, methodologically transparent study of the vendor's product outcomes — ideally third-party, or at minimum a vendor-published case study with a stated methodology, sample size, and defined ROI calculation. Even then, this would likely cap at Internal challenge only given the conflict of interest, unless verified against multiple independent client outcomes.

## Claim 3 — "Content ops teams cut cycle time by roughly a third"

**Original claim:** "Content ops teams cut cycle time by roughly a third — this is basically common knowledge at this point." (submitted by the user, referencing general industry sentiment)

**Reason excluded:** This duplicates the benchmark already captured as `BM-001` (28% cycle time reduction, Client-facing, sourced to the ContentOps Benchmark Institute 2023 report) but with weaker, unsourced evidence ("roughly a third," "common knowledge"). Rather than create a second, weaker row for the same underlying claim, this version is excluded in favor of the better-evidenced row already in the register.

**Missing fields:** All of them — this is an unsourced restatement of a claim that already has a properly sourced counterpart.

**What would promote this:** Nothing further needed — see `BM-001` in the register, which already covers this benchmark with full sourcing. If this "roughly a third" figure comes from a genuinely different study than the one behind BM-001, resubmit it with its own source so the two can be compared as distinct data points rather than treated as duplicates.

## Claim 4 — Approval-cycle rework rate

**Original claim:** User requested a benchmark for approval-cycle rework rate to support a client narrative. No evidence was supplied for this specific metric.

**Reason excluded:** No verified benchmark exists for this metric at any precedence tier — not in the user-supplied evidence, not in `default-industry-benchmark-register.csv` (which currently holds no real approved rows — see that file), and no promotable candidate in `benchmark-sourcing-backlog.md` either.

**Missing fields:** All fields — there is no evidence trail to check for this specific metric yet.

**What would promote this:** Any primary, dated, scoped source for approval-cycle rework rate — from the user directly, or added to the default industry register after passing the ten-condition test.

**Fallback wording for client deliverables:**

> No verified benchmark was provided for this claim. The analysis therefore uses client-owned baseline data only and should be treated as directional until a verified benchmark is supplied.
